# Summary: Defense & Aerospace Stocks

## Abstract

The five major U.S. defense primes -- Lockheed Martin (LMT), RTX Corporation (RTX), Northrop Grumman (NOC), General Dynamics (GD), and L3Harris Technologies (LHX) -- all sit on record backlogs totaling over $700 billion combined as of year-end 2025, and are positioned to benefit significantly from an Iran-US/Israel war through accelerated munitions orders, missile defense demand, supplemental defense appropriations, and foreign military sales to Gulf allies. However, valuations are historically stretched (P/E ratios of 22-40x versus historical averages of 15-20x), meaning a substantial "war premium" is already priced in, and the risk of a short-lived conflict causing a sharp valuation correction is the primary downside. Historical precedent from the Gulf War (1991), Iraq War (2003), Soleimani strike (2020), and Israel-Hamas conflict (2023-24) shows that defense stocks spike 5-11% on Day 1 of conflict, but sustained outperformance depends on whether the conflict drives a fundamental increase in baseline defense budgets rather than just emergency supplementals.

## Key Insights

- **Record Backlogs Across the Board**: RTX leads with $268B backlog, followed by LMT at $194B, GD at $118B, NOC at $95.7B, and LHX at $30B+. All five companies have book-to-bill ratios above 1.0, signaling continued expansion. [Source: RTX Corp Q4 2025 Earnings - https://www.rtx.com/news/news-center/2026/01/27/rtx-reports-2025-results-and-announces-2026-outlook] [Source: Fintool - https://fintool.com/news/lockheed-martin-q4-record-backlog-missile-ramp]

- **Historical Pattern: Immediate Spike, Then Divergence**: Defense stocks consistently rally 5-11% in the first trading session after conflict onset (Oct 9, 2023: NOC +11.4%, LMT +8.9%, GD +10%, RTX +4.6%). Sustained outperformance requires protracted conflict -- the Israel-Hamas war delivered RTX +82.7% and LMT +54.9% over 12 months, while the 2020 Soleimani spike was short-lived. [Source: AOAV - https://aoav.org.uk/2023/stock-prices-of-major-defence-companies-surge-in-wake-of-october-7th-attacks-in-israel/] [Source: Responsible Statecraft - https://responsiblestatecraft.org/october-7-hamas-attack/]

- **Valuations Are Stretched**: P/E ratios range from ~22.5x (GD) to ~40x (RTX) versus 5-year averages of 15-20x. LMT trades at ~21-30x (depending on GAAP vs adjusted), well above its historical ~17x. This limits upside and amplifies downside if the conflict de-escalates quickly. [Source: MacroTrends - https://www.macrotrends.net/stocks/charts/LMT/LOCKHEED%20MARTIN/pe-ratio] [Source: YCharts - https://ycharts.com/companies/RTX/pe_ratio]

- **Missile Defense Is the Key Catalyst**: Patriot (RTX), THAAD (LMT), SM-3/SM-6 (RTX), and Iron Dome (RTX co-production) interceptors are the most directly demanded systems. LMT has signed agreements to quadruple THAAD interceptor production to 400/year, and the Pentagon is pushing to double or quadruple overall missile output. [Source: Fintool - https://fintool.com/news/lockheed-martin-q4-record-backlog-missile-ramp] [Source: ROIC.ai - https://www.roic.ai/news/pentagon-pushes-defense-contractors-to-dramatically-ramp-up-missile-production-09-29-2025]

- **B-21 Raider Acceleration Benefits NOC**: Northrop Grumman received a $4.5B injection in February 2026 to accelerate B-21 Raider stealth bomber production, driven by the need for deep-penetrating strike capabilities against Iranian hardened/underground targets. This is a distinct catalyst not yet fully priced in. [Source: Breaking Defense - https://breakingdefense.com] [Source: Air & Space Forces Magazine - https://airandspaceforces.com]

- **Executive Order Creates Binary Risk (Jan 2026)**: A Trump Executive Order (Jan 7, 2026) prohibits buybacks and dividends for defense contractors deemed "underperforming" on production speed. RTX (Pratt & Whitney supply chain issues) and LMT (F-35 delays) are most at risk of penalties, while GD (submarines meeting schedule) appears safest. [Source: Sidley Austin LLP - https://sidley.com]

- **GD Has Best Value Profile**: General Dynamics trades at the lowest P/E (~22.5x), has the healthiest balance sheet (1.2x net debt/EBITDA), the highest FCF yield (5.4%), and the safest dividend (35% payout ratio, 26 consecutive years of increases). Its $118B record backlog is driven by submarine programs (Virginia-class, Columbia-class) and Gulfstream jets. [Source: MetricDuck - https://www.metricduck.com/blog/defense-contractor-roic-rankings-2025] [Source: Investing.com - https://za.investing.com/news/company-news/general-dynamics-fy-2025-presentation-doubledigit-growth-amid-record-backlog-93CH-4083039]

- **Foreign Military Sales Will Accelerate**: Gulf states (Saudi Arabia, UAE) and Israel will aggressively purchase U.S. air defense and aircraft. Saudi Arabia's pending THAAD deal (~$15B), potential F-35 sales to Gulf allies, and Israel's requests for additional interceptors would benefit LMT and RTX most. [Source: Gemini research, multiple defense industry sources]

- **Supply Chain Constraints Limit Ramp Speed**: Despite surging demand, rocket motors, microchips, energetics, and skilled labor remain bottlenecks. Doubling or quadrupling production takes 12-24 months of lead time, meaning revenue could be back-loaded and margins may suffer from expedite costs and overtime. [Source: ROIC.ai - https://www.roic.ai/news/pentagon-pushes-defense-contractors-to-dramatically-ramp-up-missile-production-09-29-2025]

- **Conviction Ranking**: Both Gemini and OpenAI sources converge on NOC and GD as the best risk/reward picks (less stretched valuations, distinct catalysts), with LMT as the safe core holding and RTX as the highest-beta/highest-risk pure play on conflict. LHX offers niche upside via Aerojet Rocketdyne but carries integration risk.
