# Summary: Gold & Precious Metals

## Abstract

Gold is trading near $5,200/oz on February 28, 2026, the day US-Israeli strikes on Iran commenced, after hitting an all-time high of $5,595 on January 29. The precious metals complex is a primary beneficiary of the conflict through safe-haven demand, central bank accumulation (863 tonnes in 2025), and structural de-dollarization. Major bank targets range from $5,400 (Goldman Sachs) to $6,300 (J.P. Morgan) for year-end 2026, with an upside scenario of $8,000+ if private allocation normalizes. Mining stocks (NEM, AEM, GOLD) are generating record margins at $3,000-3,900/oz above AISC, but face rising input costs from war-driven energy inflation. Silver at ~$85/oz benefits from dual safe-haven and military/industrial demand with a structural supply deficit. Best risk-adjusted plays are WPM (streaming model immune to cost inflation), AEM (safe-jurisdiction miner with lowest AISC), and GLD (pure gold exposure without operational risk).

## Key Insights

- **Gold Price at Inflection Point**: Gold spot at ~$5,200/oz after reaching $5,595 ATH in January. JP Morgan targets $6,300 year-end; Goldman Sachs at $5,400; UBS bull case $7,200. Analysts warn a direct US-Iran military conflict could push gold 15% higher to $5,500-$5,800 within two weeks. [Source: https://www.jpmorgan.com/insights/global-research/commodities/gold-prices] [Source: https://www.kitco.com/news/article/2026-02-24/gold-could-hit-5800-if-us-iran-conflict-escalates-natixis-dahdah]

- **January 2026 Crash as Key Risk Precedent**: Gold crashed 12% in a single day on January 31, 2026, from $5,595 to ~$4,800, triggered by the Warsh Fed nomination and compounded by CME margin hikes (gold margins raised from 6% to 8%). Silver fell 31.4% in the same session -- the largest one-day decline since 1980. This demonstrates that even in a bull market, leveraged positioning can cause violent forced liquidations. [Source: https://alexlexington.com/blogs/news/gold-and-silver-crash-january-2026-what-caused-fridays-historic-drop] [Source: https://www.cnbc.com/2026/02/02/gold-silver-fall-further-as-cme-margin-hike-stokes-selling.html]

- **Central Bank Structural Bid**: Central banks bought 863 tonnes of gold in 2025 and are on pace for ~800 tonnes in 2026. Nearly 70% of central banks plan to increase gold reserves in the next 5 years. The global official gold reserves exceeded US Treasury foreign holdings ($386B vs $382B) for the first time in Q2 2025. Dollar's share of FX reserves fell to 56.3%, a 30-year low. [Source: https://www.gold.org/goldhub/research/gold-demand-trends/gold-demand-trends-full-year-2025/central-banks] [Source: https://www.man.com/insights/views-from-the-floor-2025-april-29]

- **Newmont (NEM) -- Largest Miner, Record FCF**: Trading at ~$125-130, P/E ~19x. 2026 guidance: 5.3M oz at $1,680/oz AISC. Record $7.3B free cash flow in 2025. This is a "trough year" for production (down 10% from 5.9M oz). At $5,200 gold, margin is ~$3,520/oz. BofA raised target to $151; Jefferies to $158. Key risk: operational complexity from Newcrest integration. [Source: https://www.newmont.com/investors/news-release/news-details/2026/Newmont-Reports-Fourth-Quarter-and-Full-Year-2025-Results-Provides-2026-Guidance-and-Announces-Enhanced-Capital-Allocation-Framework/default.aspx] [Source: https://www.marketbeat.com/instant-alerts/bank-of-america-issues-positive-forecast-for-newmont-nysenem-stock-price-2026-02-28/]

- **Barrick Gold (GOLD/B) -- Value Play With Geopolitical Risk**: Trading at ~$50, P/E ~17x. 2026 guidance: 2.9-3.25M oz at $1,760-$1,950/oz AISC (highest among peers). Q4 2025 AISC was $1,581/oz, with 2026 guided higher due to cost inflation. Pristine balance sheet but highest geopolitical exposure (Africa, Middle East). Fourmile resource doubled in Q4. Best value on a P/E basis but riskiest operationally. [Source: https://www.barrick.com/English/news/news-details/2026/q4-2025-results/default.aspx] [Source: https://markets.financialcontent.com/stocks/article/marketminute-2026-2-27-the-gilded-era-newmont-and-barrick-gold-reap-super-margins-as-gold-shatters-5000-milestone]

- **Agnico Eagle (AEM) -- "Swiss Franc" of Gold Miners**: Trading at ~$246, P/E ~24x (premium). 2026 guidance: 3.3-3.5M oz at $1,400-$1,550/oz AISC -- lowest among majors. Operates exclusively in safe jurisdictions (Canada, Finland, Australia). Q4 revenue grew 60% YoY to $3.56B. Plans 20-30% production growth over the next decade to 4M+ oz. At $5,200 gold, margin is ~$3,700/oz. [Source: https://seekingalpha.com/article/4870591-agnico-eagle-stock-top-quality-miner-built-new-gold-reality] [Source: https://finviz.com/news/310049/agnico-eagle-mines-aem-q4-earnings-and-revenues-beat-estimates]

- **Wheaton Precious Metals (WPM) -- Inflation-Proof Streamer**: Trading at ~$161, P/E ~72x (premium for business model). Fixed purchase costs of ~$473/oz gold, ~$5.75/oz silver through 2029. 77% net profit margin in Q3. Zero exposure to rising mining input costs (diesel, labor). Production growing to 870K GEO by 2029, 950K by 2030-2034. No debt, $1.2B cash. In a war scenario with spiking energy costs, WPM's margins expand purely with commodity price while miners' margins get compressed. [Source: https://seekingalpha.com/article/4857779-wheaton-precious-metals-streaming-high-margin-silver-and-gold-profits] [Source: https://www.wheatonpm.com/portfolio/portfolio-overview/default.aspx]

- **Silver's Dual Demand Tailwind**: Silver at ~$85/oz with gold/silver ratio at 58:1 (compressed from 80+:1, signaling mature bull market). J.P. Morgan projects $81/oz average in 2026, GoldSilver expects $100+. Structural supply deficit of 160-200M oz annually. Solar PV consuming 120-125M oz/year; military applications (avionics, missiles) add price-insensitive demand. SLV at ~$85 with 174% 1-year return. PAAS at $68 with BofA target of $84. [Source: https://www.jpmorgan.com/insights/global-research/commodities/silver-prices] [Source: https://goldsilver.com/industry-news/article/silver-price-forecast-predictions/]

- **GLD as Capital Preservation Core**: SPDR Gold Shares (GLD) at ~$481, AUM $180.65B, expense ratio 0.40%. YTD return 16.7%. Provides pure gold exposure without operational, jurisdictional, or cost inflation risk. During the January crash, GLD fell with gold but had no additional equity-specific risk layered on. Ideal for investors who want war premium exposure without mining stock volatility. [Source: https://www.ssga.com/us/en/intermediary/etfs/spdr-gold-shares-gld]

- **Miners Historically Underperform Bullion Long-Term but Offer Leverage**: Over 2006-2025, GDX returned 26% total vs GLD's 373%. However, gold miners offer ~1.7x beta to gold in rallies. In 2026 YTD, miners outperformed gold (Philadelphia Gold & Silver Index +15% vs gold futures +6%). Risk: miners correlate with equities in panics (March 2020 effect). Mining stocks are twice as volatile as gold. [Source: https://www.fool.com/coverage/etfs/2026/01/21/gdx-vs-gldm-gold-miners-with-leverage-or-direct-gold-price-exposure/] [Source: https://quantpedia.com/golds-rally-and-the-gold-mining-stocks-trap/]

- **Historical War Pattern: "Buy the Rumor, Sell the News"**: In the 1990 Gulf War, gold rallied ~15% pre-invasion but corrected once Desert Storm proved swift. In 2003 Iraq War, gold peaked in Feb at $388 then dropped 10-15% within 30 days of invasion. The 2020 Soleimani strike saw gold spike briefly to $1,600 then retreat within weeks. The current Iran conflict is more comparable to 1979-1980 (prolonged, involving major oil producer) than 1991 (swift). If war becomes protracted, gold sustains. If quick resolution, war premium ($200-300/oz) evaporates. [Source: https://www.lbma.org.uk/alchemist/issue-30/not-just-another-war-story] [Source: https://www.herobullion.com/how-do-wars-affect-gold-prices/]

- **Key Risk: Margin Call Cascade and Deflationary Liquidation**: If war causes oil to spike above $150/bbl, triggering global recession and equity market crash, gold may be sold to cover margin calls on other assets (as in 2008 and briefly in March 2020). Gold typically falls last and recovers first, but initial drawdown can be 10-15%. The January 2026 crash demonstrated this vulnerability: leveraged longs were liquidated by CME margin hikes, creating a cascading sell-off. A sudden de-escalation or diplomatic breakthrough would also cause rapid unwinding of the war premium. [Source: https://alexlexington.com/blogs/news/gold-and-silver-crash-january-2026-what-caused-fridays-historic-drop] [Source: https://www.marketpulse.com/markets/market-reactions-morning-volatility-explodes/]
