# Summary: Emerging Next-Generation UUV Programs

## Abstract

The next-generation UUV market is undergoing a paradigm shift from small, short-endurance systems to large and extra-large platforms capable of autonomous operations lasting weeks to months. The market is bifurcating between "exquisite" multi-mission systems (Boeing Orca, ~$274M for 5 units) and "attritable" mass-producible systems (Anduril Ghost Shark, $1.1B AUD program). Major operational milestones are clustered in 2024-2026, with Ghost Shark entering production, Orca beginning at-sea testing, Manta Ray completing full-scale demonstrations, and China revealing 40m+ XXL UUVs at Sanya naval base.

## Key Insights

- **Anduril Ghost Shark dominates the emerging XLUUV space**: Went from concept to a $1.7B AUD ($1.1B USD) program of record in under five years, with first prototype delivered to the Royal Australian Navy in April 2024 (one year ahead of schedule). Uses 3D-printed hulls and Lattice OS for collaborative autonomy. Full-rate production negotiations announced August 2025 with entry into service early 2026. [Source: breakingdefense.com, navalnews.com]

- **Anduril Dive-LD selected for US Replicator initiative**: Commercial-off-the-shelf large displacement UUV at ~$2.5M per unit, selected for DoD Replicator Tranche 2 in 2024. Rated to 6,000m depth with 10+ day endurance. [Source: defensescoop.com]

- **Boeing Orca XLUUV is the largest Western UUV program**: 26m long, 85 tons, with a massive 34-foot modular payload bay (8-ton capacity) and 6,500nm range. Prototype XLE0 delivered Dec 2023; first production unit XLE1 testing began late 2024. Total development value ~$274M for 5 vehicles. Designed primarily for mine-laying and ASW. [Source: navalnews.com, defensemirror.com]

- **Northrop Grumman Manta Ray pioneers energy harvesting**: DARPA-funded biomimetic glider design completed full-scale in-water testing off Southern California in early 2024. Features thermal gradient energy harvesting for theoretically unlimited endurance, seafloor hibernation mode, and ships in five standard containers. Transitioning to US Navy. [Source: darpa.mil, therobotreport.com]

- **MEDUSA introduces expendable offensive mining UUV concept**: General Atomics/Bluefin Robotics contract ($15.9M base, up to $58M total) for a torpedo-tube-launched, one-way mining delivery system. Designed for clandestine offensive operations in denied areas. Risk reduction testing completed early 2026. [Source: gdmissionsystems.com, navy.mil]

- **UK's XV Excalibur (Project Cetus) is Europe's largest uncrewed submersible**: MSubs 12m, 17-tonne XL-UUV launched Feb 2025 and handed to the Royal Navy for trials. Fits in a standard shipping container at cost of just Â£15.4M. Intended for ASW barrier operations and seabed warfare. [Source: fw-mag.com, naval-technology.com]

- **France's Naval Group developing combat-designated UCUV**: Explicitly designated as an Unmanned Combat Underwater Vehicle, with a framework agreement signed Dec 2023 and demonstrator ordered Jan 2024. Features Controlled Decision-making Autonomy for communications-denied combat environments. [Source: navalnews.com, fw-mag.com]

- **Kongsberg HUGIN Endurance achieves shore-to-shore autonomy**: 11.9m AUV with 1,200nm range and 15-day endurance at 6,000m depth. First production system completed factory acceptance in early 2024. Eliminates need for a mother ship. HUGIN Superior accepted by US Navy (DIU) Feb 2025. [Source: kongsberg.com, rovplanet.com]

- **China's XXL UUVs represent largest known unmanned submarines**: Two vehicles >40m in length observed at Sanya naval base in 2024/2025, significantly larger than Boeing Orca. Torpedo-shaped with pump-jet propulsion; theorized to have intercontinental range (10,000+ nm). Strategic intent debated between ISR, blockade running, and offensive operations. [Source: navalnews.com, rfa.org]

- **L3Harris Iver4 achieves torpedo tube launch and recovery**: First demonstrated in 2023, this capability transforms any standard submarine into a UUV mothership. AUKUS Pillar 2 "Project Scylla" demonstrated this with the Royal Navy in mid-2025. [Source: oceansciencetechnology.com, asdnews.com]

- **Cellula Robotics Solus-XR pioneers hydrogen fuel cell UUV**: Canadian-developed system with 5,000km range using hydrogen fuel cells. Sea trials initiated late 2023; MASS compliance achieved Dec 2024. Targets Arctic and Pacific port-to-port surveillance. [Source: oceanroboticsplanet.com, navalnews.com]

- **South Korea developing MRXUUV with hydrogen fuel cells**: Hanwha Systems/Ocean contracted in Nov 2023 for a Mission Reconfigurable XLUUV with up to 30-day endurance using hydrogen fuel cell/AIP propulsion. Prototype due 2027, driven by North Korean submarine threat. [Source: navalnews.com, kedglobal.com]

- **Japan's ATLA/MHI developing modular 10m-class UUV**: Prototype unveiled 2023 with sea trials ongoing through 2025. Modular design expandable to 15.6m with additional sections. Current 1-week endurance with plans for AIP/diesel-electric extension. [Source: navalnews.com, navalinstitute.com.au]

- **Russia's Poseidon nuclear-armed autonomous torpedo**: Nuclear-powered with theoretically unlimited range. Putin claimed successful nuclear power unit tests in Oct 2025, though claims rely on state media and remain unverified. Strategic "doomsday" weapon class. [Source: thebarentsobserver.com, outrider.org]

### Chinese XXL UUV Propulsion and Capabilities

**Status:** Resolved (quick research)

The AJX002 designation was publicly revealed at a September 2025 military parade. The vehicle measures 18-20m with pump-jet propulsion and cruciform rudders. Officially described as optimized for mine-laying. Propulsion is assessed as diesel-electric with high-capacity lithium-ion batteries (no evidence of nuclear propulsion). At least 6 distinct XLUUV designs are undergoing testing. Two larger 40m+ variants observed at Sanya are housed in floating docks, suggesting conventional fuel systems.
